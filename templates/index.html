MOTIVATION

Fake news detection is the task of detecting forms of news consisting of deliberate disinformation or hoaxes spread via traditional news media (print and broadcast) or online social media. Although this concept is not new by any stretch of the imagination, the recent burst of internet and social media means that discriminating rumours from facts is more essential than ever. The recent surge of the Coronavirus Outbreak was a test to the existing system and it failed. Even today, as the second wave dies down in India, there are Nobel Laureates who claim that the vaccinated public will suffer a horrendous death within 2 years. When an ordinary citizen reads news like this, and even if they go the extra step and research a bit into whether the said Nobel prize winner did say such a thing, she will find out that it is indeed true. What's false, however is the claim that vaccines cause such harm. It takes more than 20 minutes of a deep dive into the perpetrator to find out that he has been against vaccines since the HIV vaccine came out decades ago. To make this easier on the general public and with an aim to demolish the spread of such nonsense, we have come up with a Natural Language Processing based News Credibility Classifier.
